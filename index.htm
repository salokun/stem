ORGANIZATION HISTORY

I welcome you to IGNITE STEM, a great organisation geared towards raising a new generation of innovative minds in the field of Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM). I owe you a responsibility to take you through a short story of how the organisation came about. In the wake of the year 2013 during the National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) programme, I took up a volunteer job with DAYSTAR CHRISTIAN CENTRE as a science tutor for students taking the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME), a necessity to securing an admission placement into polytechnics and universities in Nigeria. My volunteering lasted almost two years; amidst that I tutored several learners in the STEM fields in my private times. One major underlying challenge I came across for most of these learners was their disinterest in learning STEM; this in part stems from the teachers they have in their classrooms and on top of it was the disconnect between what is taught in the classroom and the real-world. The latter I solved by using real-world analogies to explain phenomenon and definitions. I enjoyed what I did then as a tutor trying to get students back on track to learning STEM in a more enjoyable way. The rest they say is history. Found my way to my alma Mata Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife Nigeria in the month of August 2015 to kick-start my Masters degree program, although against my wish. I could not pass on the urge to make a difference in helping students enjoy learning STEM and ultimately turning that knowledge into something viable. One evening after my spare time I resolved to organise a STEM fair and exhibition for schools around the town where our campus was located for students to showcase their built projects to some invited guests and the general public. We had a nine-member team who consulted with schools and the overall logistics that is to be involved. Among them today is Dayo Ekundayo, a strong standing member of the IGNITE STEM team today. The team met weekly mostly Sunday evenings to give reports on the consultations with schools. The big blow came in one of the meetings when it was related that none of the schools they consulted with had an idea of what the program was about, but although remarked that would be a great one to be involved in. Their lack of interest stems from the constraints of shortage of STEM teachers, financial resources and a host of others. We knew work was set for us at that point. The following year 2016, January to be precise the IGNITE STEM CLUB was born. It was born out of passion to ignite the interest of pupils and students with experiments and projects that are related to what is taught in the classroom and common place materials in a bid to help them understand the basic science taught in the classroom. The first club activities held in August 2016 at Christway Schools, Ile-Ife, since then we have gone to impact pupils and students in different schools in their tens of thousands every term and every year. (EO).

CORPORATE VISION

We live and exist to be and be recognized as the world’s leading provider of Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) education through innovative solutions that build and empower innovative minds towards a self-reliant society. We will achieve this by dedicating ourselves to providing qualitative STEM education that liberates young minds to inspire, to explore, to involve, to innovate and to lead. Over the years we have crystallized our corporate activities into three main pillars; Corporate Activities 1. To achieve scientific literacy for all irrespective of age, religion, ethnicity, race and socioeconomic class. 2. To inspire young minds to learn Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) and pursue STEM careers. 3. To raise STEM proficiency level with the goal of equipping minds to become problem￾solvers and solution thinkers to combat the pressing challenges of humanity and the global community. Also our values describe the approach and the manner in which we want to work to achieve our goals (vision). Our values represent who we are. They serve as our compass; not only do they define us, but they guide us at the same time. Together, our Vision and Values form the framework for all of our decisions and activities. They serve as both an orientation and a guideline for leadership, and also define our corporate culture. We build our organization from within, promoting and rewarding people without regard to any difference unrelated to performance. We act on the conviction that the men and women of IGNITE STEM will always be our most important asset. Our Values 1. Sustainable Profitable Growth And Performance 2. Innovation For The Success Of Our Consumers 3. Personal And Professional Competence 4. Mutual Respect And Open Dialogue 5. Integrity.
